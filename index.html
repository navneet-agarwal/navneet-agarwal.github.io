Automated Mental Health Assessment (PhD Thesis)

Supervisor: Gaël Dias

My research aims to answer three major questions in the domain of automated depression estimation; (1) the role of discourse structure in mental health understanding, (2) the relevance of input representation, and (3) the importance of medical knowledge in automated mental health analysis. This forms the basis for my research on multi-view architectures for encoding discourse structure, graph-based transcript representations for modeling non-linear interactions within dyadic conversations and insight generation, and examining the model's behavior within the framework of clinical annotations to draw parallels with their predictive tendencies, thus enhancing the reliability of neural network models as predictive tools in healthcare.

Variational Inference based mini-batch learning on graphs (M.Tech Thesis)

Supervisors: Prof. Raju K. George and Prof. Sumitra S.

My M.Tech thesis focused on Deep Learning based architectures defined for processing graph based data. My work mainly focused on combining Variational auto-encoders, Graph Convolution Networks (GCN) and Attention models in order to define an architecture that significantly reduced the memory requirement for processing graph based datasets by providing a mini-batch mode learning for the data. The architecture was implemented using pytorch and was able to give significant improvements over the state of the art graph kernel methods for two datasets (Enzymes and Proteins). The proposed model achieved the same prediction performance as traditional GCN in 50 epochs as compared to 500 epochs. The model also allows training in mini-batch setting, enabling deployment on systems with memory constraints.
